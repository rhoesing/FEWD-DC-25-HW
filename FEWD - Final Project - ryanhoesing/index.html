Let me tell you a little about me

Growing up in the midwest teaches one a lot about life. Hard work, perseverance, and humility are all values I learned to love while growing up in Omaha, Nebraska. Since I can remember I have always questioned the status quo and done my best to find answers to the most difficult of life’s questions. This has lead me to far ends of the globe, allowing me to experience what many Nebraskans can only ever imagine. My perseverance led me to serve as a Peace Corps volunteer in West Africa, which in turn led me to continue my public service as a federal employee in a technology office. My curiosity has lead me to ponder how all things work and if I don’t know shouldn’t I learn how they work. This website is representation of my curiousity.

What I do

Education

Growing up in Omaha it is hard not to be an avid Nebraska football fan. My loyalty to the Huskers may have helped influence my college decision. After 4 years at an all boys Jesuit College Preparatory High School, I decided to attend the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. After dabbling in architecture my first year, I decided it was a better fit for me to study business as I found it more applicable in the real world workplace. I graduate with a B.A. Business Administration in 2011.

Design

I love the arts! I began drawing at a very young age. It was not until later in high school that I used other mediums like charcoal, acrylic & oil paints, and balsa wood to push my designs to greater heights. Below you will find some of the artwork I have created throughout the years. I find creating a website to be one of the most challenging pieces of work to date. Design and structure are reliant on one another.

Globetrotter

I did not find my love for travel until my early college years. After taking a year off between my sophomore and junior year of college to live in Hawaii and then meander around Europe for a while I decided to come back to Nebraska to finish my degree so I could eventually make enough money to continue to globetrott the world. Post college I decided to spend 2 years of my life devoted to helping those less fortunate than myself while at the same time getting a cultural experience one can only get by living in another country for several years.
